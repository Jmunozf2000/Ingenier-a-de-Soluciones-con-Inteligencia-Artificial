FAA Advisory Circular AC 43.13-1B - Acceptable Methods, Techniques, and Practices
Aircraft Inspection and Repair - Chapter 11: Aircraft Electrical Systems

Section 11-113: Wire Splicing Requirements
Wire splices in aircraft wiring must maintain the original electrical characteristics of the circuit.
Splices shall not be used in locations subject to continuous movement or flexing.
Each splice must be mechanically secure before soldering or crimping.
Use only approved splicing connectors or terminals that match the wire gauge (AWG).
The splice must restore the original insulation rating of the wire.
A maximum of one splice per wire segment between termination points is recommended.
Splices must be accessible for inspection per maintenance requirements.

Section 11-114: Crimping Requirements
Crimping tools must be of the proper type and size for the terminal and wire gauge being used.
The crimping tool M22520/2-01 with positioner M22520/2-09 is approved for AWG 20 wire.
After crimping, perform a pull test: minimum 40 lb pull force for AWG 20 terminals.
Terminal type MS20659-225 is approved for use with AWG 20 wire.
Inspect the crimp for cracks, splits, or improper deformation before installation.

Section 11-115: Wire Identification and Protection
All wire must be identified per the aircraft wiring diagram.
Wire bundles must be properly supported and protected from chafing.
Minimum bend radius for wire bundles is 10 times the bundle diameter.
